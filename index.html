SIR MODEL TO EXPLAIN
COVID-19

Describe : This project involves the development of a mathematical model to analyze the spread of COVID-19 using the Susceptible-Infected-Recovered (SIR) model, implemented in Python. The SIR model is a compartmental framework in epidemiology that categorizes the population into three distinct groups: Susceptible (S), Infected (I), and Recovered (R). The model illustrates the progression of an epidemic by explaining that the number of infected individuals will initially increase as the virus spreads through the susceptible population. As more individuals become infected and eventually recover, the number of susceptible individuals decreases, leading to a decline in new infections. Ultimately, the model predicts that the epidemic will peak and then subside as the majority of the population transitions to the recovered category, thereby reducing the pool of susceptible individuals and halting further spread.

LSTM TO PREDICT COVID-19
COVID-19

Describe : This project explores the application of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, a specialized form of recurrent neural networks (RNNs), to predict the number of COVID-19 cases over time. LSTM networks are particularly well-suited for time series forecasting due to their ability to capture long-term dependencies and temporal patterns in sequential data.In the context of COVID-19 case prediction, LSTM networks are trained on historical data, including previous case counts, and other relevant features, to model the underlying trends and dynamics of the pandemic. The network’s architecture, which includes memory cells, input gates, forget gates, and output gates, enables it to selectively retain or discard information over time, thereby addressing the vanishing gradient problem often encountered in traditional RNNs
